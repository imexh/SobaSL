"Soba Sri Lanka-2022"
Tree Planting Campaign

Goals

Prior to initiating the "Soba Sri Lanka-2022" tree planting campaign, the Green Army club of SLTC in correlation with the Research and development committee will be carrying out extensive research to explore problems related to the "Kadolana" reserve, and draw in appropriate solutions.

Goal 1

Planting of 2000 mangrove saplings (Sonneratia caseolaris), which have very important environmental and economic value.

Goal 2

Nurturing the newly planted saplings until they can survive on its own.


Goal 3

Educating the general public on the importance and value of mangrove trees.

About

One of the primary objectives of the SLTC Green Army, is to give its maximum support to increase the forest density of the country. Accordingly, the reconstruction and regaining of destroyed forests, reserves and sanctuaries in Sri Lanka is in accordance with the cabinet draft, 5/2001 of Sri Lanka. Therefore, the Green Army club of SLTC will be joining hands with the forest department, Sri Lanka to increase the percentage of forest cover of Sri Lanka with a group of more than 500 volunteer environmental pilots who aspire to provide their maximum direct and indirect efforts in making this objective a reality.

Introduction

The Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka is regarded as a country with an agriculture orientated economy. Historically Sri Lanka was often renowned as the "green paradise" by foreign visitors.

In 1982, 82% of Sri Lanka was covered solely by forests. But over time, as the country became increasingly urbanized and industrialized, its natural forest cover gradually began to deplete. According to a 2019 forest density report, Sri Lanka has only 16.5% of its total forest cover left, with 1.14% percentage reduction of forest cover per annum. This is a very imminent and serious threat to the country, and its ecosystems.

Deforestation leads to devastation; the value of one tree is beyond our comprehension. The Green Army club of the Sri Lanka Technological Campus, which is endowed with this value, is embarking on a quest to increase the forest density in the Senapola Reserve situated in the Kalpitiya Peninsula in the Puttalam District, North Western province, the site of the 'Soba Sri Lanka - 2022' tree planting campaign.
